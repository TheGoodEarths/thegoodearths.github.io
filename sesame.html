Sesame oil

Sesame seeds, as many of you are aware, is a powerful natural health food and a welcome addition to your diet. It is wide spread in cultivation and the oil extracted from the seeds are used almost universally for food preparation. First grown in the Indus valley of India where the word oil in many of the dialects and the Tamil language refers directly to the sesame seed oil. Today Sesame oil is considered sacred and used in various religious ceremonies. Sesame oil is also used in preparation of Ayurvedic medicines. It is an excellent moisturizer for skin and hair. Sesame seed oil is now produced in China and in the Middle East, and other developing countries. But, there is a worldwide shortage of the seeds which drives the price high.

How we produce sesame oil

Sesame seed oil is produced by the pressing and crushing the seeds to release their oil. As you might guess, a large number of seeds(3 kgs) are needed to produce a liter of oil. The darker brown oil is produced from toasted sesame seeds.

Cold pressed oil is the most natural and is still made using methods dating back to centuries. These methods while producing a rich golden oil with vital nutrients is not the most efficient way to extract the oil.
